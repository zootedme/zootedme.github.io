### **What is Google Adsense and How Does It Work?**

Google AdSense is a Google program that allows publishers to monetize their websites, blogs, and YouTube videos by showing advertisements. Google pays you when a visitor clicks on the ad or when ad impressions are reached, depending on the type of ad.

Text, graphics, video, and interactive media can all be used in adverts. An automated ad auction system selects these adverts. The auction picks the highest bidder from among Google AdWords users who are bidding.

Google uses a variety of targeting methods to choose the ads that appear on your site:

* **Personalized targeting** — for example, advertisers can choose sites that cater to a specific category (musical theater lovers).
* **Run of Network targeting** – this option includes all AdSense network sites except those that are explicitly excluded.
* **Contextual targeting** — they look at the keywords used, the frequency of the terms, and the link structure to figure out the context of the website and match the adverts to it.
* **Ad placement targeting**-  enables marketers to determine where their ads will show. Advertisers who believe your site is a good fit for their target demographic choose these adverts.

### **Why Does Google Choose the Advertising Through an Auction?**

Advertisers on the Google Display Network compete for the right to have their adverts seen on AdSense. Advertisers are ranked in the ad auction based on their bid and Quality Score. As a result, you'll attract advertisers who are eager to pay the most and provide excellent service.

### **Are There Any AdSense Account Requirements? How do I submit my application?**

Google chooses which sites are eligible for an AdSense account based on 12 characteristics about you and your site:

**You**

* You must own and maintain **control of your website**.
* You are not permitted to utilize methods that **generate false traffic**.
* You must be at least **18 years old**.

**Your Website**

* Your website's content must be **distinctive and interesting**.
* The content must **adhere** to [Google's Content Guidelines](https://support.google.com/adsense/answer/9335564).
* You can apply for Google AdSense if your site is hosted by a host partner, such as Blogger or YouTube, but you must meet other [eligibility conditions](https://support.google.com/adsense/answer/50288).
* The **site's navigation** must be simple.
* You are not allowed to publish **plagiarized content**.
* Your website should have been up for at least **6 months**.
* You'll need a **lot of traffic**.
* A website with a **history of abuse** (fake messages, deceptive activities) is ineligible to apply.

### **Adsense Ads Come in a Range of Sizes**

What kinds of adverts are appropriate for your website? You can choose from a range of ad formats in Google AdSense:

* **Text advertisements:** These are the advertising that is displayed in the form of basic text when you search for something on Google. They have a title, a brief description, and a website link. These advertisements could display on your site or in Google searches.
* **Display ads:** These are the advertisements that appear on web pages. The three basic formats are as follows:  **Horizontal, Banner, Skyscraper, Wide skyscraper, Square, Small square (200 x 200)**
* **Rich Media Ads:** These interactive ads can be in the form of an image, a video, or HTML. There are three different sorts of advertisements:

1. **Dynamic image ads:** These are advertisements that consist of animated text that appears as the user scrolls down the page. You have the option of selecting a size and shape: vertical, horizontal, or square.
2. **Video ads:** Google offers various different forms of video ads:

* a. **Standard  :** up to 20 seconds of non-skippable commercials. This can show up in the pre-roll, mid-roll, and post-roll sections of the game.
* b. **TrueView advertising :** allow users to skip the video after five seconds. They can be found in the pre-roll and mid-roll slots, but they must adhere to additional rules.

### **How to Make Your First Ad Unit?**

What is an ad unit, exactly? A single piece of **AdSense ad code** can result in one or many Google adverts. On their AdSense account, users may [create, manage, and edit ad units](https://support.google.com/adsense/answer/9274025?visit_id=637311899964539835-912497761&rd=1). There are a variety of ad units to choose from:

* Responsive and customizable display adverts
* In-feed advertising is native adverts that appear within a feed.
* Native ads that appear between portions of an article are known as in-article ads.
* Matched content — advertisements that direct visitors to the content.
* Link — displays appropriate links that enhance the page's content.

#### **So, how do you create your first ad unit? Let's have a look.**

To begin, go into your **AdSense account** and navigate to the **Ads tab**. Select the option by ad unit and Display advertising from your overview page. Make a name for your unit so you can find it later.

For your ad unit, you have numerous options:

* You can choose whether you want it to be responsive (which we encourage!)
* You have the option of choosing the size.

After you save, you'll be **given the code to copy and paste into your pages**' tags. Check out Google's [code implementation](https://support.google.com/adsense/topic/9274018) instructions if you're not sure how to do it. Click Done, and the adverts will appear on the page in a few minutes.

### **How Do You Get Paid on Adsense?**

You should start receiving money from Google every month once your AdSense account and ad units are up and operating. Every month, if your profits approach or exceed **$100**, Google will pay you. You are paid directly by the company, either via direct transfer into your bank account or by check (where available).

**What happens if you don't make $100 in a given month?** Earnings will continue to accumulate until you reach your goal.

### **The Pros and Cons of Monetizing with Google AdSense**

Let's look at the pros and cons of AdSense now that you know the basics.

**Pros**

* You can use a single account to monetize several websites.
* Because it's supposedly passive income, you shouldn't have to put in a lot of effort.
* It's completely free.
* You don't have to develop your own ads because Google will do it for you.

**Cons**

* To make a fair quantity of money, you need a lot of traffic.
* Too many advertisements may irritate your visitors.
* To be paid, you must earn at least $100.
* If you're using AdSense, you can't utilize another ad network.
* Policy violations can result in your account being blocked.

### **What to Do (and What Not to Do!) When Making Money With Adsense**

AdSense demands a strategy and adherence to the guidelines in order to generate consistent earnings. Here's what you should and shouldn't do when monetizing your site with AdSense, whether you're new to AdSense or already have an account and want to improve your AdSense Ad Revenue:

* **Adhere to Google policies:** read and adhere to the Google Publisher policies, AdSense program policies, and webmaster policies.
* **Do not click** on your own advertisements.
* **Don't pay per click:** offering click incentives, purchasing Pay Per Click (PPC) space, or any other method of rewarding clicks violates Google policy.
* **Provide valuable material:** providing valuable content to your target audience leads to them clicking on advertising and earning money.
* **Increase organic traffic:** improve your site for SEO and optimize your content to attract organic visitors.
* **Make sure your site is mobile-friendly:** this will allow your target audience to find you no matter where they are.
* **Improve the ad's structure and format** by placing advertisements above the fold, experimenting with ad types and placement, and placing a leaderboard ad next to your brand.
* **In-Content Ads:** Use native advertisements that fit in with the content: native ads that blend in with the content have a better probability of being spotted and clicked on.
* Check your outcomes and adjust your plan accordingly by **monitoring**, **analyzing**, and **refining** your strategy.
