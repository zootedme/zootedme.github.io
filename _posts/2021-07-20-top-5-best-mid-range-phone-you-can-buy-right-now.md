Although not everyone can afford a flagship phone like the Samsung Galaxy S21 Ultra or the iPhone 12 Pro, the good news is that there are many less expensive options that provide an outstanding experience.

Don't panic if your bank account is looking a little bare and you need a new smartphone; some are half the price or even less. The top mid-range phones available today have been tested and ranked.

On a SIM-free basis, a mid-range phone costs between $350, the upper limit of our even cheaper budget phones chart, and £600/$850, making it ideal for pairing with a SIM-only plan.

Anything more expensive will be found in our overall best smartphone list, which focuses on flagship phones, as well as our pick of the best Android phones.

Mid-range smartphones are designed to offer flagship-level functionality at a lower price. You may have to give up a little on one or more features, such as the camera or build quality, but saving hundreds of dollars is often well worth it - and some of the phones at the top of the price range are flagships in their own right.

Realme, Xiaomi, and Poco phones are excellent alternatives, but keep in mind that many of these don't release in the United States or Canada, where OnePlus, Motorola, and Google are stronger options. For additional information on buying a mid-range phone, continue reading past our rundown.

### **Things to consider when buying a mid-range phone**

Because it's impossible to define a mid-range phone by its specifications, we've instead used a pricing bracket.

Some mid-range phones will take an all-around good-value approach, with capable specs in each area; others will focus on a key trait, such as the camera or display, and promise flagship-rivaling capabilities in that one area; and still others will have been flagships in the past, so will offer fantastic specs at a fantastic price, but may be running on slightly older hardware.

One thing to keep in mind is that you'll almost certainly have to forego features like wireless charging or waterproofing, which are still reserved for the most costly phones on the market.

Chinese phones are a terrific mid-range option since they combine excellent specifications with a lower price than you'd expect - but unfortunately, just a few are available in the United States. You may have heard of Xiaomi, Realme, and OnePlus for their incredible prices, but there are a slew of other lesser-known Chinese brands that can also deliver.

There is one mid-range iPhone on the market - the [iPhone SE](https://www.apple.com/iphone-se/) from 2020 - but we don't believe it offers excellent value when compared to Android competitors. Consider it if you know you'll need an iPhone, but stay away from it otherwise.

Keep in mind that it's not always about the numbers. Although these phones may not be as speedy as the latest flagships, they will almost definitely satisfy the majority of consumers. Choose a phone that strikes a mix between price, performance, features, and design that appeals to you and your needs.
